# How to use this guide

This handbook is designed to work both as a structured study course and as an ongoing operational reference. How you use it will depend on where you are in your career.

---

## If you are studying for the role

Work through the modules in order. Each module builds on the one before it. The suggested sequence is:

1. Read Module 1 to establish your understanding of the regulatory framework.
2. Work through Module 2 to understand what a shift actually looks like before you study the individual skills within it.
3. Study Modules 3 and 4 in depth — these cover the ARO and WSO qualifications that form the technical core of the role.
4. Review Module 5 on radio, even if you hold an AROC already. The airport context is different from an airborne context.
5. Read Modules 6, 7, and 8 before your first week on the job, not after.

After each module, test yourself using the scenario exercises in the reference section. These are written to reflect real situations you will encounter.

---

## If you are already in the role

Use the search function to find specific procedures, definitions, or checklists. The reference section contains a full glossary and index of CASA document references. The scenario exercises are useful for self-assessment and shift briefings.

---

## Callout boxes

Throughout the handbook you will see the following callout types:

> 📋 **CASA reference** — Links to the specific regulation or MOS standard that applies to the topic being discussed.

> ⚠️ **Important** — Operational or safety-critical information that requires particular attention.

> 💡 **Operational tip** — Practical guidance from experienced operators that is not written into the regulations but is considered standard practice.

> 🔁 **Scenario** — A worked example or practice question based on a realistic operational situation.

---

## Keeping this handbook current

Aviation regulations are updated regularly. CASA publishes amendments to Part 139 and the associated MOS through the Federal Register of Legislation. When a significant change occurs, the relevant section of this handbook will be updated and the version number incremented.

You can check the current version of Part 139 at [legislation.gov.au](https://www.legislation.gov.au) and CASA advisory circulars at [casa.gov.au](https://www.casa.gov.au).

---

## Printing

Each module can be printed as a standalone document. Checklists in the reference section are formatted for A4 printing and lamination for use on shift.
