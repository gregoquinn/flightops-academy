# The Australian aviation system

Australia's aviation system is one of the most tightly regulated in the world. Understanding its structure — who is responsible for what, and under what authority — is essential before you can make sense of any individual procedure or regulation.

---

## The three pillars

Australian civil aviation operates under three main organisations, each with a distinct role:

| Organisation | Role | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| **CASA** | Safety regulator | Civil Aviation Act 1988 |
| **Airservices Australia** | Air navigation service provider | Airspace Act 2007 |
| **Airport operators** | Aerodrome certification and operation | CASR Part 139 |

As a Flight Operations Officer, you work for the airport operator. You are accountable to CASA's standards and you coordinate daily with Airservices Australia. Understanding the boundary between these organisations is important — particularly when something goes wrong.

---

## The legislative framework

Australian aviation law flows down from the Commonwealth through several layers:

```
Civil Aviation Act 1988
        ↓
Civil Aviation Regulations 1988 (CAR)
        ↓
Civil Aviation Safety Regulations 1998 (CASR)
        ↓
Manual of Standards (MOS)
        ↓
Advisory Circulars (AC)
```

**Civil Aviation Act 1988** — the primary Act of Parliament. Establishes CASA, defines its powers, and sets the framework for aviation safety regulation in Australia.

**Civil Aviation Safety Regulations 1998 (CASR)** — subordinate legislation made under the Act. Part 139 of the CASR governs aerodromes. This is the document that your aerodrome certification is based on.

**Manual of Standards Part 139 (MOS 139)** — the technical detail behind the regulations. Where CASR Part 139 says what must be done, the MOS says how it must be done and to what standard. Most of your day-to-day procedures will be traceable to MOS 139.

**Advisory Circulars (ACs)** — guidance material published by CASA. Not legally binding, but they represent CASA's interpretation of how the regulations should be met. Following the AC is the safest way to demonstrate compliance.

## ICAO's role in the Australian system

Australia is a signatory to the Convention on International Civil Aviation (the Chicago Convention) and is therefore bound by the Standards and Recommended Practices (SARPs) published by ICAO.

For aerodromes, the relevant ICAO documents are:

- **Annex 14 — Aerodromes:** Sets international standards for aerodrome design, equipment, and operations.
- **Doc 9981 — PANS-Aerodromes:** Procedures for Air Navigation Services related to aerodrome operations.

CASA is required to comply with ICAO Annex 14 standards and to notify ICAO of any differences between Australian regulations and the Annex 14 standard. These differences are published in the AIP Australia.

> 📋 **CASA reference:** Differences between Australian regulations and ICAO Annex 14 are listed in AIP Australia, GEN 1.7.

## The airport operator's responsibility

Under CASR Part 139, a certified aerodrome operator has a legal obligation to:

- Maintain the aerodrome in a safe and serviceable condition
- Conduct regular inspections of the movement area
- Manage works on or near the movement area safely
- Report changes in aerodrome conditions via NOTAM
- Respond to emergencies in accordance with the Airport Emergency Plan
- Maintain records of inspections, incidents, and maintenance

As a Flight Operations Officer, you are the person who carries out these obligations on a daily basis. The airport operator is ultimately accountable, but you are the one on the ground.

---

## The difference between a certified and registered aerodrome

Not all aerodromes in Australia are certified under Part 139.

| Type | Requirements | Typical examples |
|------|---|---|
| **Certified aerodrome** | Full Part 139 compliance, aerodrome manual, CASA audit | RPT airports, international airports |
| **Registered aerodrome** | Basic registration, reduced obligations | Private strips, helicopter landing sites |

Norfolk Island Airport is a **certified aerodrome** and is subject to the full requirements of CASR Part 139.
