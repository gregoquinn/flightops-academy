# Airservices Australia

Airservices Australia is a Commonwealth government-owned corporation that provides air traffic management and related services across Australia. It is separate from CASA — where CASA regulates safety, Airservices delivers operational aviation services.

---

## What Airservices does

Airservices Australia provides:

- **Air traffic control (ATC)** — separation of aircraft in controlled airspace and at controlled aerodromes
- **Aeronautical information services** — the AIP, NOTAMs, and aeronautical charts
- **Aviation rescue and fire fighting (ARFF)** — at major Australian airports
- **Communication and navigation infrastructure** — VOR, ILS, NDB, and other navigation aids

At a small certified aerodrome like Norfolk Island, Airservices has a more limited presence than at a major airport. There is no resident ATC or ARFF service on Norfolk Island — this is one of the key reasons the Flight Operations Officer role exists there.

---

## Air traffic services and the FOO

At an aerodrome without a resident ATC unit, aircraft communicate with the relevant en-route centre and/or area approach control. On Norfolk Island, the controlling authority is **Brisbane Centre.**

As a Flight Operations Officer, you are not an air traffic controller. You do not provide separation services. However, you do:

- Monitor the Common Traffic Advisory Frequency (CTAF) or Mandatory Broadcast Zone (MBZ) as applicable
- Provide aerodrome information broadcasts (wind, runway in use, known traffic)
- Coordinate with Brisbane Centre for emergency declarations
- Relay information between aircraft and ground services when required

> ⚠️ **Important:** There is a critical distinction between providing aerodrome information and providing ATC services. You are legally permitted to do the former. The latter requires an ATC licence. Never give instructions that imply separation authority.

## The AIP — Aeronautical Information Publication

The **AIP Australia** is published and maintained by Airservices Australia. It is the authoritative source for operational information about Australian airspace and aerodromes. The AIP consists of three main parts:

| Part | Contents |
|---|---|
| **GEN** | General — administrative information, differences from ICAO standards |
| **ENR** | En route — airspace structure, routes, procedures |
| **AD** | Aerodromes — individual aerodrome charts and information |

The aerodrome entry for Norfolk Island Airport is in AIP AD. It contains the published runway dimensions, lighting specifications, operating hours, and contact information. As a FOO you should be familiar with the Norfolk Island AD entry and know when to check it for updates.

> 📋 **CASA reference:** The AIP is maintained by Airservices and is available at [airservicesaustralia.com/aip](https://www.airservicesaustralia.com/aip).

## NOTAMs

**NOTAM** stands for Notice to Air Missions (previously Notice to Airmen). NOTAMs are short operational notices distributed through the global NOTAM system that advise pilots of temporary changes to aerodrome or airspace information.

As a Flight Operations Officer, you will both read and (in the WSO role) create NOTAMs.

NOTAMs are filed through the **NAIPS** (National Aeronautical Information Processing System) web portal, maintained by Airservices Australia.

Key NOTAM types you will deal with:

| NOTAM trigger | Example |
|---|---|
| Runway closure | Runway 11/29 closed for line marking — use 03/21 |
| Lighting unserviceable | Runway edge lights 09 threshold unserviceable |
| Works in progress | Crane operating within obstacle limitation surface |
| Threshold change | Runway 11 threshold displaced 150m |
| Wildlife hazard | Increased bird activity in vicinity of aerodrome |

## ATIS — Automatic Terminal Information Service

At some aerodromes, Airservices provides an **ATIS** — a recorded broadcast of current aerodrome information (wind, QNH, runway in use, NOTAM summary). Norfolk Island does not have an ATIS. The FOO provides equivalent information through voice broadcasts on the CTAF/MBZ.

---

## ARFF — Aviation Rescue and Fire Fighting

At major Australian airports, Airservices provides ARFF services — the crash fire trucks you see near the runway. At smaller certified aerodromes including Norfolk Island, ARFF is not provided by Airservices. The aerodrome operator is responsible for making appropriate emergency arrangements.

On Norfolk Island, fire response is provided through the local Norfolk Island Fire Service under a standing arrangement with the aerodrome operator, coordinated through the Airport Emergency Plan.

> 💡 **Operational tip:** Know your local emergency service contacts cold. On an island with limited resources, the few minutes saved by not having to look up a phone number in a crisis can matter significantly.
