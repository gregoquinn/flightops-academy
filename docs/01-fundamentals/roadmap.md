# Study roadmap

The following four-week plan is designed for someone working full-time who can dedicate one to two hours per day to study. Adjust the pace to suit your schedule.

---

## Week 1 — Framework and foundations

The goal of week one is to understand the system you are working within before you study any operational procedures.

| Day | Topic | Pages |
|-----|-------|-------|
| 1 | Australian aviation system overview | Module 1 |
| 2 | CASA — structure, powers, Part 139 | Module 1 |
| 3 | Airservices Australia — role and services | Module 1 |
| 4 | ICAO — Annex 14 and Doc 9981 | Module 1 |
| 5 | Daily shift overview — what a day looks like | Module 2 |
| 6–7 | Review and self-test — scenario exercises 1–5 | Reference |

**Week 1 outcome:** You understand who regulates aerodromes, what the key documents are, and what happens during a typical shift.

---

## Week 2 — Aerodrome Reporting Officer

The ARO skills are the most technically detailed part of the role. Take your time here.

| Day | Topic | Pages |
|-----|-------|-------|
| 8 | Morning brief and opening inspections | Module 2 |
| 9 | Runway inspection — methodology and reporting | Module 3 |
| 10 | Lighting systems — types, checks, failures | Module 3 |
| 11 | FOD management — identification and response | Module 3 |
| 12 | Pavement assessment — defects and reporting | Module 3 |
| 13–14 | Review and self-test — scenario exercises 6–12 | Reference |

**Week 2 outcome:** You can conduct a runway inspection, identify lighting defects, manage a FOD incident, and document pavement defects to the MOS Part 139 standard.

## After the four weeks

The handbook does not end at week four. Return to specific sections before your first time handling each situation type. The scenario exercises in the reference section will continue to be useful for shift briefings and self-assessment throughout your career.

> 💡 **Operational tip:** The most experienced airport operations officers keep their study notes accessible on shift for the first six months. There is no shame in checking a procedure. The shame is in not checking.
