# About this handbook

FlightOps Academy is a professional reference and study guide for Airport Operations Officers working at certified aerodromes in Australia and on Norfolk Island. It covers the knowledge required to operate as a Flight Operations Officer, Aerodrome Reporting Officer (ARO), and Works Safety Officer (WSO) under the framework established by CASA Part 139.

---

## Who this is written for

This handbook is written for candidates who already hold an aviation background — commercial pilots, UAV operators, or those with experience in aviation safety or operations — and are transitioning into an airport-side role. It assumes you already understand basic aviation terminology, airspace, and regulatory culture. It does not teach you to fly; it teaches you what happens on the ground.

If you are new to aviation entirely, the glossary and fundamentals module will give you the foundation you need before moving into the operational chapters.

---

## What this handbook covers

The handbook is organised into eight modules, each corresponding to a key area of airport operations:

**Module 1 — Aviation fundamentals**
The structure of the Australian aviation system, the roles of CASA and Airservices Australia, and the ICAO standards that underpin everything.

**Module 2 — Daily airport operations**
What actually happens during a shift. Morning checks, aircraft movements, documentation, and closing procedures.

**Module 3 — Aerodrome Reporting Officer**
Runway and taxiway inspections, lighting systems, FOD management, and pavement assessment. The core technical skills of the ARO role.

**Module 4 — Works Safety Officer**
Planning and supervising airside works, contractor management, NOTAM creation, and risk assessment.

**Module 5 — Radio communications**
AROC licensing, standard phraseology, emergency frequencies, and worked examples of common radio calls.

**Module 6 — Emergency management**
Response procedures for aircraft accidents, fuel spills, medical evacuations, wildlife strikes, and other declared emergencies.

**Module 7 — Norfolk Island Airport**
Airport-specific content including runway characteristics, local weather patterns, wildlife species, and procedures unique to the island.

**Module 8 — Aviation security**
ASIC requirements, airside access control, restricted area management, and the Drug and Alcohol Management Plan.

## Regulatory basis

All content is written with direct reference to the applicable Australian and international standards:

| Document | Issued by | Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| CASR Part 139 | CASA | Primary regulation for aerodromes |
| MOS Part 139 | CASA | Technical standards for aerodrome operations |
| ICAO Annex 14 | ICAO | International aerodrome standards |
| ICAO Doc 9981 | ICAO | PANS-Aerodromes procedures |
| AIP Australia | Airservices Australia | Published operational procedures |
| CASA Advisory Circulars | CASA | Guidance material |

Where this handbook refers to a regulatory requirement, the source document is noted. You are encouraged to read the source material directly — this handbook explains and contextualises the regulations, but it does not replace them.

---

## A note on Norfolk Island

Norfolk Island Airport operates under the same CASA regulatory framework as mainland Australian aerodromes, with some practical differences arising from its remote location, limited resources, and unique environment. Module 7 documents these differences in detail. Where procedures in earlier modules differ on Norfolk Island, this is noted in the text.
