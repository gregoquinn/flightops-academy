# Aircraft accident

An aircraft accident on or near the aerodrome is the highest-consequence emergency you may face. The procedures below are based on MOS Part 139 and standard Australian airport operations practice. Always follow your specific AEP, which will contain site-specific detail.

---

## Definition

An **aircraft accident** (ICAO definition) is an occurrence associated with the operation of an aircraft in which a person is fatally or seriously injured, the aircraft sustains damage or structural failure, or the aircraft is missing or completely inaccessible.

A **serious incident** is one involving circumstances indicating that an accident nearly occurred. Both are reportable to the ATSB.

---

## Immediate actions — aircraft accident on aerodrome

These actions are sequential priorities. Do them in this order.

### 1. Receive notification or observe the accident

You may be notified by:
- Direct radio transmission from the aircraft
- Observation from the operations room or movement area
- Call from emergency services or a witness
- Pilot report after an abnormal landing

### 2. Activate emergency services — immediately

Call 000. State:
- "Aircraft accident at Norfolk Island Airport"
- Location of the aircraft (as precisely as you can)
- Aircraft type
- Estimated number of persons on board
- Nature of the emergency (fire, crash, gear collapse, etc.)

Simultaneously or immediately after — activate the airport emergency services and notify the airline.

### 3. Acknowledge and communicate with the aircraft (if possible)

If the aircraft is still on the frequency:

> *"[Callsign], Norfolk Radio, emergency services activated, ETA [approximate]. Runway [number] clear."*

### 4. Clear the runway and movement area

Ensure no other aircraft are on approach or the runway. If there is inbound traffic, broadcast immediately:

> *"All stations Norfolk, Norfolk Radio. Aircraft emergency on runway [number]. Runway closed. All aircraft hold or divert. Advise intentions."*

### 5. Guide emergency services

When fire trucks or ambulances arrive, direct them to the aircraft location. Have the access route ready — know which gate to open and the fastest route to each part of the movement area.

### 6. Establish an Emergency Coordination Point

Position yourself where you can see the aircraft and communicate with emergency services, without being in their way. Typically this is the operations room or a designated control point in the AEP.

### 7. Notify the chain

- Airport manager
- Airline duty manager
- Brisbane Centre / Airservices
- ATSB (1800 011 034) — **mandatory for accidents and serious incidents**

### 8. Preserve the scene

Once life safety actions are complete, preserve the accident scene. Do not move wreckage, remove debris, or allow unauthorised access. The ATSB will investigate and they need the scene intact.

Establish a perimeter:
- Cones or barriers around the accident area
- No access without ATSB or police authority
- No photography by aerodrome staff

### 9. Document continuously

From the moment you become aware of the emergency, write everything down with times:
- Time of notification / observation
- Nature of emergency
- Actions taken and times
- All communications
- Arrival times of emergency services
- Names of persons at the scene

This record is critical for the ATSB investigation and any subsequent legal or regulatory proceedings.

## Aircraft accident off the aerodrome

If an aircraft crashes in the vicinity of the aerodrome but off airport property:

1. Call 000 — this is now primarily a police and emergency services matter
2. Notify the ATSB
3. Notify Brisbane Centre
4. Keep the aerodrome available for emergency service helicopters or medical aircraft that may need to use it
5. Assist with communication as requested by emergency services

---

## Post-accident operations

After an on-aerodrome accident, the aerodrome will be partially or fully closed. Before any movement area can be reopened:

1. ATSB must have completed their on-scene investigation (or authorised you to reopen)
2. Wreckage must be cleared by the authorised recovery contractor
3. The movement area must be inspected and confirmed clear
4. A NOTAM must be issued for reopening
5. Management must formally authorise the reopening

Do not reopen any area of the movement area involved in an accident without formal authority from the ATSB and your airport management.

---

## ATSB notification obligations

The following occurrences must be reported to the ATSB:
- Any accident involving injury to persons or damage to aircraft
- Any serious incident (near-miss, runway incursion, bird strike causing damage, gear collapse, etc.)

Report to ATSB: **1800 011 034** (24 hours)
Online: [atsb.gov.au](https://www.atsb.gov.au)

> 📋 **CASA reference:** Transport Safety Investigation Act 2003 — sets out mandatory reporting obligations. ATSB Investigation Manual — available at atsb.gov.au.
