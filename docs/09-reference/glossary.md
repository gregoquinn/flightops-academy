# Glossary

Key terms used in airport operations, this handbook, and Australian aviation regulations. Where a term has a specific regulatory definition, that definition is noted.

---

## A

**AIP** — Aeronautical Information Publication. The official publication maintained by Airservices Australia containing procedural, regulatory, and descriptive information about Australia's aviation system. Divided into GEN, ENR, and AD sections.

**AMSL** — Above Mean Sea Level. Standard vertical reference for altitude and elevation.

**APN** — Apron. The paved area at an aerodrome used for aircraft parking, loading, refuelling, and maintenance.

**ARFF** — Aviation Rescue and Fire Fighting. The fire service provided at airports. At major airports provided by Airservices Australia; at smaller airports by the aerodrome operator's arrangement.

**ARO** — Aerodrome Reporting Officer. A person qualified and authorised to conduct movement area inspections and report on aerodrome condition.

**AROC** — Aviation Radio Operator Certificate. Required to operate aeronautical radio equipment in Australia. Issued by CASA.

**ASIC** — Aviation Security Identification Card. The security card required to access the secure areas of a security controlled airport. Issued under the Aviation Transport Security Act 2004.

**ASDA** — Accelerate-Stop Distance Available. The declared distance available for an accelerating aircraft to abort a take-off and come to a stop.

**ATIS** — Automatic Terminal Information Service. A continuous recorded broadcast of current aerodrome information.

---

## B

**Backtrack** — The procedure by which an aircraft taxies back along the runway after landing (or before take-off) to reach a taxiway or holding point.

---

## C

**CASR** — Civil Aviation Safety Regulations 1998. The principal Australian aviation regulations, made under the Civil Aviation Act 1988.

**CASA** — Civil Aviation Safety Authority. Australia's independent aviation safety regulator.

**CTAF** — Common Traffic Advisory Frequency. A designated radio frequency used by aircraft in the vicinity of an aerodrome without an operating ATC unit. Broadcasts are advisory, not clearances.

---

## D

**DAMP** — Drug and Alcohol Management Plan. A plan required under CASR Part 99 for certain aviation industry participants.

**Declared distances** — The four distances published for a runway: TORA, TODA, ASDA, and LDA. Used by pilots for take-off and landing performance calculations.

---

## F

**FOD** — Foreign Object Debris (or Foreign Object Damage). Any object on the movement area that is not supposed to be there and could cause damage to an aircraft.

**FOO** — Flight Operations Officer. The person responsible for the operational management of an aerodrome during a shift.

---

## G

**GPU** — Ground Power Unit. A portable electrical power source used to supply power to an aircraft on the ground without running its engines or APU.

---

## I

**ICAO** — International Civil Aviation Organisation. The United Nations specialised agency for civil aviation. Publishes Standards and Recommended Practices (SARPs) through the Annex to the Chicago Convention.

---

## L

**LDA** — Landing Distance Available. The declared distance available for an aircraft to land and come to a stop.

---

## M

**MBZ** — Mandatory Broadcast Zone. A designated area of airspace around certain aerodromes where radio broadcasts are mandatory for all aircraft.

**METAR** — Meteorological Aerodrome Report. A standardised format weather report for an aerodrome, issued at regular intervals (typically hourly).

**MOS** — Manual of Standards. Detailed technical standards made under CASR. MOS Part 139 contains the aerodrome standards.

**Movement area** — The runways, taxiways, and aprons of an aerodrome used for aircraft operations.

---

## N

**NOTAM** — Notice to Air Missions. A notice containing information essential to personnel concerned with flight operations, not known sufficiently in advance to be published by other means.

---

## O

**OLS** — Obstacle Limitation Surface. The three-dimensional surfaces around an aerodrome within which obstacles must not penetrate.

---

## P

**PAPI** — Precision Approach Path Indicator. A system of lights near the runway threshold that provides visual glidepath guidance to landing aircraft. Correct approach is indicated by 2 red and 2 white lights.

**PCN** — Pavement Classification Number. A number expressing the bearing strength of a pavement for unrestricted operations. Published in the AIP AD for each runway.

---

## Q

**QNH** — The altimeter setting that causes an aircraft altimeter to read aerodrome elevation when on the ground at that aerodrome. Published in METARs and TAFs.

---

## R

**RPT** — Regular Public Transport. Scheduled airline services carrying passengers or cargo for hire or reward.

**RWY** — Runway.

---

## S

**SIGMET** — Significant Meteorological Information. A weather advisory concerning weather significant to the safety of aircraft operations, including thunderstorms, turbulence, icing, and volcanic ash.

---

## T

**TAF** — Terminal Area Forecast. A weather forecast for an aerodrome, typically covering a 24 or 30 hour period.

**TODA** — Take-off Distance Available. The declared distance available for an aircraft to complete its take-off run and achieve an initial climb to 35 feet.

**TORA** — Take-off Run Available. The declared length of runway available for the ground run of a taking-off aircraft.

**TWY** — Taxiway.

---

## W

**WSO** — Works Safety Officer. A person qualified and authorised to supervise works on or near the movement area of a certified aerodrome.

---

## Y

**YSNF** — The ICAO four-letter aerodrome designator for Norfolk Island Airport. Used in flight plans, NOTAMs, and AIP references. Y = Australia; S = Queensland/NSW/ACT/Norfolk Island; NF = Norfolk.
