# Sample radio calls

The following examples cover the most common radio exchanges you will have as a FOO at Norfolk Island Airport. Read each one, then practise saying it aloud until it sounds natural.

---

## Opening the aerodrome

**Self-broadcast on opening:**

> *"All stations Norfolk, Norfolk Radio, aerodrome open for operations, runway 11 in use, wind 110 at 10 knots, QNH 1014. Norfolk Radio."*

---

## Responding to an inbound aircraft

**Pilot:** *"Norfolk Radio, Golf Foxtrot Uniform November Kilo, 30 miles northeast, inbound for landing, request aerodrome information."*

**FOO:** *"Golf Foxtrot Uniform November Kilo, Norfolk Radio, runway 11 in use, wind 100 at 08 knots, QNH 1015, no known traffic. PAPI available runway 11. Report final."*

**Pilot:** *"Runway 11, QNH 1015, will report final, Golf Uniform November Kilo."*

---

## Traffic information to an inbound aircraft

**FOO:** *"Golf Uniform November Kilo, Norfolk Radio, traffic information, Fokker 50 departed runway 29 two minutes ago, tracking northwest, now 10 miles northwest, report sighting or runway in sight."*

---

## Pilot reports final

**Pilot:** *"Norfolk Radio, Golf Uniform November Kilo, final runway 11."*

**FOO:** *"Golf Uniform November Kilo, Norfolk Radio, runway 11 clear, wind 100 at 10 knots."*

---

## Post-landing — after normal touchdown

*No transmission required unless the aircraft calls you or there is something to report.*

If the pilot calls:

**Pilot:** *"Norfolk Radio, Golf Uniform November Kilo, on the ground, request taxi instructions."*

**FOO:** *"Golf Uniform November Kilo, Norfolk Radio, taxy via Alpha to the apron, give way to [other traffic if applicable]."*

---

## Departing aircraft

**Pilot:** *"Norfolk Radio, VH-XYZ, request departure information, tracking Sydney."*

**FOO:** *"VH-XYZ, Norfolk Radio, runway 11 in use, wind 100 at 10 knots, QNH 1015, no known traffic. Departure your discretion. Report airborne."*

**Pilot:** *"Runway 11, QNH 1015, VH-XYZ."*

*After departure:*

**Pilot:** *"Norfolk Radio, VH-XYZ, airborne runway 11, tracking Sydney."*

**FOO:** *"VH-XYZ, Norfolk Radio, roger, safe flight."*

---

## Vehicle entry to movement area

**FOO:** *"All stations Norfolk, operations vehicle, runway inspection, entering runway 11 at taxy Alpha, [time]. Notify of any inbound traffic."*

*On completion:*

**FOO:** *"All stations Norfolk, operations vehicle, runway inspection complete, vacating via taxy Alpha, runway clear."*

---

## FOD on runway — broadcast

**FOO:** *"All stations Norfolk, Norfolk Radio, runway temporarily unavailable, FOD removal in progress. Expect [estimated delay]. Advise intentions."*

*When clear:*

**FOO:** *"All stations Norfolk, Norfolk Radio, runway 11 clear, available for operations."*

---

## Wildlife on runway — warning to inbound

**FOO:** *"Golf Uniform November Kilo, Norfolk Radio, bird activity on runway 11 vicinity threshold. Dispersal in progress. Anticipate clear by your ETA but monitor."*

**Pilot:** *"Roger, monitoring, Golf Uniform November Kilo."*

*If birds cannot be dispersed in time:*

**FOO:** *"Golf Uniform November Kilo, Norfolk Radio, significant bird activity on runway 11, advise if you wish to orbit while we complete dispersal, or accept the information and proceed."*

---

## Unserviceable lighting — broadcast on pilot query

**Pilot:** *"Norfolk Radio, Lima Foxtrot Whiskey, on approach runway 29, PAPI appears unserviceable."*

**FOO:** *"Lima Foxtrot Whiskey, Norfolk Radio, confirm, PAPI runway 29 unserviceable, NOTAM issued [number], standard visual approach in effect, wind 280 at 06, runway 29 clear."*

---

## Works vehicle — requesting crossing of runway

**Works vehicle:** *"Norfolk Radio, crane truck, request clearance cross runway 11 to worksite."*

**FOO:** *[Check no inbound traffic, visually confirm runway clear]* *"Crane truck, Norfolk Radio, no known traffic, runway 11 clear, cross runway 11 and hold short of taxy Alpha. Report clear."*

**Works vehicle:** *"Crossing runway 11, crane truck."*

*After crossing:*

**Works vehicle:** *"Crane truck, clear runway 11, holding short taxy Alpha."*

**FOO:** *"Crane truck, Norfolk Radio, roger."*

---

## Mayday — receiving

**Pilot:** *"Mayday, Mayday, Mayday. VH-ABC. Engine failure. 15 miles southeast, descending. 3 persons on board, 1 hour fuel."*

**FOO:** *"VH-ABC, Norfolk Radio, Mayday acknowledged. Runway 11 in use, 1,951 metres, runway clear. Emergency services being activated. Pass your intentions."*

---

## Medical evacuation — inbound coordination

**FOO broadcast:**

> *"All stations Norfolk, Norfolk Radio, medical evacuation aircraft inbound, ETA [time]. Runway 11 in use, all non-essential traffic hold clear of movement area. Norfolk Radio."*

*To the medevac aircraft:*

**FOO:** *"[Callsign], Norfolk Radio, runway 11 in use and clear, wind 090 at 06, QNH 1013. Ambulance standing by apron. Report final."*

---

## Closing the aerodrome

**FOO broadcast:**

> *"All stations Norfolk, Norfolk Radio, Norfolk aerodrome closing [time] local. After-hours contact [phone number]. Norfolk Radio."*

---

## Radio check

**FOO:** *"Norfolk Radio, radio check on [frequency]."*

If someone responds: *"[Your callsign], Norfolk Radio, reading you [strength and clarity]."*

**Readability scale:**
1 — Unreadable
2 — Readable now and then
3 — Readable but with difficulty
4 — Readable
5 — Perfectly readable

A standard radio check response: *"Reading you 5, Golf Alpha Bravo."*
