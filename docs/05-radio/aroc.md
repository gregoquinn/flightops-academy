# AROC and radio licensing

The Aviation Radio Operator Certificate (AROC) is the licence required to operate aeronautical radio equipment in Australia. If you hold a commercial pilot licence or any other CASA aviation licence that includes radio endorsement, you likely already meet this requirement. This chapter confirms what is required and explains how it applies in the airport operations context.

---

## Regulatory basis

> 📋 **CASA reference:** CASR Part 64 — Radio operator certificates. The AROC is issued under CASR Part 64 by CASA.

---

## What is the AROC?

The AROC is a certificate that authorises the holder to operate aeronautical mobile radio equipment — essentially any radio used for air-to-ground or ground-to-ground communication in the aviation context. This includes:

- VHF radios in the operations room
- Vehicle-mounted aeronautical radios
- Handheld aeronautical radios
- HF radio if applicable

You must hold an AROC (or be supervised by an AROC holder) to legally operate any of this equipment.

### Who needs an AROC?

| Role | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Flight Operations Officer | AROC required |
| Aerodrome Reporting Officer | AROC required for radio use on movement area |
| Works Safety Officer | AROC required for radio contact with aircraft |
| Contractor airside (vehicle radio) | AROC required or supervised by AROC holder |

If you already hold an Australian CPL, ATPL, or any licence with a radio endorsement, contact CASA to confirm whether a separate AROC is required or whether your licence satisfies the requirement.

### Obtaining an AROC

If you do not already hold the AROC:
1. Study the AROC training material (available through CASA or approved training providers)
2. Pass the AROC written examination
3. CASA issues the certificate — it does not expire but can be suspended or cancelled

## Radio equipment on the movement area

When operating a vehicle on the movement area, your radio is your lifeline. Before entering the movement area:

- Confirm the radio is on and on the correct frequency
- Conduct a radio check
- Ensure the volume is set so you can hear transmissions clearly, including with the engine running
- Do not adjust the radio while the vehicle is moving near the runway

If your radio fails while on the movement area:
1. Move clear of the runway and taxiway immediately
2. Use a handheld backup radio if available
3. If no backup is available, use your mobile phone to call the operations room
4. Do not remain on the movement area without a functioning radio — exit and report the failure

> 💡 **Operational tip:** Always carry a charged handheld radio as a backup when conducting movement area inspections. Radio failure at the wrong moment is one of the most dangerous situations an ARO can face.

---

## Legal obligations

Operating aeronautical radio equipment without an AROC (or appropriate licence) is an offence under the CASR. More practically, an operator without an AROC may not understand correct phraseology, may transmit incorrectly in an emergency, and is an unacceptable risk in an airside environment.

Ensure your AROC is current and on record with your employer before you begin radio operations.
