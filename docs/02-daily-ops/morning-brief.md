# Morning brief and opening checks

The opening of an aerodrome for operations is a structured process, not simply a matter of arriving and switching on the lights. Every element of the opening sequence serves a purpose. Done correctly, it gives you — and the pilots who will use the aerodrome — confidence that the movement area is safe before any aircraft moves.

---

## Before you leave the operations room

Before stepping outside, complete the following desk checks. These take no more than ten minutes and are done while conditions are fresh and any issues can be assessed before the inspection begins.

### Weather assessment

Pull the current METAR for YSNF (if available) or the nearest reporting station. Check:

- **Wind:** Direction and speed. This determines runway in use. Note any gusts.
- **Visibility:** Is it above VMC minimums for the expected operations?
- **Cloud:** Ceiling height. Is it above circuit height?
- **Temperature and dewpoint:** A small spread (less than 3°C) indicates fog risk.
- **QNH:** Record this — you will need to broadcast it.
- **Trend:** Is the weather improving or deteriorating?

Check the TAF for the next 6–12 hours. Note any forecast significant changes — thunderstorms, low cloud, strong winds — that will affect operations during your shift.

> 💡 **Operational tip:** On Norfolk Island, pay close attention to the dewpoint spread in the early morning. Sea fog can develop rapidly when surface temperature drops to dewpoint. If the spread is less than 2°C at dawn, have a fog plan ready.

### NOTAM review

Log into NAIPS and retrieve all NOTAMs applicable to YSNF. Read each one. For each active NOTAM, note:

- What it affects (runway, taxiway, lighting, airspace)
- When it expires
- Whether it requires you to take any action or brief any aircraft

File your NOTAM review in the shift log with the time you checked.

### Traffic schedule

Obtain the day's movement schedule from the airline or ground handling agent. Note:

- Expected arrivals and departures with ETAs/ETDs
- Aircraft types
- Any special flights (charter, medevac, freight, military)
- Fuel orders

Build a mental picture of when your busiest periods will be.

## The opening inspection sequence

The opening inspection is a systematic check of the entire movement area. The sequence below is the recommended order — always follow your aerodrome manual if it specifies a different sequence.

### Step 1 — Vehicle check

Before entering the movement area, inspect your inspection vehicle:

| Item | Check |
|---|---|
| Tyres | Inflated, no visible damage |
| Fuel | Sufficient for the inspection |
| Radio | Operational, correct frequency selected |
| Lights | All lights functional, amber beacon working |
| Hi-vis vest | On and visible |
| Inspection forms | Blank forms available |
| Camera or phone | For photographing defects |
| FOD collection bag | Empty and accessible |
| Emergency equipment | First aid kit, fire extinguisher as per vehicle checklist |

### Step 2 — Radio check

Before entering the movement area, conduct a radio check on the CTAF:

> *"Norfolk Radio, [vehicle callsign], radio check on [frequency]."*

Confirm you have a working radio. If you cannot raise a response, do not assume the radio is working — check it before proceeding. A vehicle on the runway with no radio is a hazard.

### Step 3 — Entry broadcast

When ready to enter the movement area:

> *"All stations Norfolk, [vehicle callsign], runway inspection, entering runway [designation] at [time], [frequency]."*

Pause and listen for any traffic. If there is traffic, wait until it is clear. If the aerodrome is a Mandatory Broadcast Zone, your broadcast is legally required.

### Step 4 — Runway inspection (full length, both sides)

Drive the full length of the runway on one side, then return on the other. You are looking for:

**Surface defects**
- New cracking or joint failure
- Settlement or rutting
- Surface ravelling or aggregate loss
- Rubber deposits at touchdown zone
- Water pooling or drainage issues
- Any foreign objects

**Markings**
- Centreline — continuous, white, visible
- Threshold bars — all present, white
- Aiming point markings — visible at 300m from each threshold
- Touchdown zone markings — all bars present within first 900m
- Runway designator — legible from the approach direction

**Lighting**
Inspect lighting at dawn or in low light conditions when lamps are on and defects are visible. On a pre-dawn inspection, drive slowly and check:
- All edge lights are present and illuminated
- No consecutive lights are unserviceable
- Threshold lights show green from approach direction
- Runway end lights show red from the landing direction
- PAPI units are illuminated and appear correctly aligned
- No lights are damaged, tilted, or obscured by grass

**Wildlife**
Note any wildlife on or adjacent to the runway. Record species, numbers, and location. Take dispersal action if required.

**Runway strip and shoulders**
- No erosion into the graded strip
- No objects projecting above strip level
- Drainage channels clear

### Step 5 — Taxiway inspection

Inspect the taxiway system:
- Surface condition
- Taxiway edge markings visible (yellow)
- Hold short markings at runway intersections clearly visible
- Taxiway lighting operational
- No FOD

### Step 6 — Apron inspection

Inspect the apron:
- Surface condition and drainage
- Parking bay markings visible
- Apron lights operational
- No unauthorised vehicles or objects
- Fuel storage area — no leaks, security intact
- Ground equipment — stored correctly, not near aircraft movement paths

### Step 7 — Threshold areas

Inspect the area beyond each runway threshold:
- No obstacles in the approach or departure surfaces
- Approach lighting — clean and operational
- No wildlife concentrations in the undershoot area

### Step 8 — Aerodrome beacon

Confirm the aerodrome beacon is operational if it is operating (typically dusk to dawn, or when visibility requires it). The beacon at YSNF alternates white and green (civil land aerodrome).

## Completing the inspection

When the inspection is complete:

**Exit broadcast:**
> *"All stations Norfolk, [vehicle callsign], runway inspection complete, vacating [exit point], [time]."*

**Document the inspection immediately.** Complete the runway inspection form while the details are fresh. Every defect must be recorded with:
- Location (e.g., "Left edge light, 400m from threshold 11")
- Nature (e.g., "Lamp unserviceable — dark")
- Severity (hazard / significant / minor)
- Action taken

**Report defects.** If any defects require a NOTAM, issue it before the first aircraft movement. If defects require maintenance, notify maintenance and record the notification in the shift log.

**Open the aerodrome.** Record the time the aerodrome is open for operations in the shift log. Activate any systems that are not already operational.

## Morning brief for RPT operations

Before the first RPT aircraft arrives, it is good practice to have the following information ready to broadcast or provide on request:

| Information | Source |
|---|---|
| Runway in use | Wind direction and strength |
| Surface wind | Current METAR or observation |
| QNH | Current METAR |
| NOTAM summary | NAIPS review |
| Any wildlife activity | Your inspection |
| Runway condition | Your inspection |
