# Closing procedures

Closing the aerodrome is as important as opening it. A properly closed aerodrome is secure, documented, and ready to be handed over or reopened at short notice. Corners cut on closing become problems on the next shift.

---

## When to begin closing

Begin the closing sequence after the last confirmed aircraft movement of the day. If there is any possibility of a late arrival (medevac, diverted aircraft, late charter), confirm with the airline or relevant authority before committing to close.

If an unscheduled arrival is expected, delay closing until it is complete.

---

## Closing sequence

### Step 1 — Final movement area inspection

Conduct a full closing inspection of the movement area in the same format as the morning opening inspection. This inspection serves two purposes:

1. To confirm the movement area is safe and secure
2. To establish the condition of the aerodrome at close of operations — your inspection record is the starting point for the next shift

Look specifically for:
- Any FOD introduced during the day's operations
- Any new pavement damage
- Any items left on the apron by ground handlers or contractors
- Any wildlife on the movement area at dusk (birds are often active at this time)
- Any lighting defects that developed during the day

### Step 2 — Lighting configuration

Set lighting to the appropriate after-hours configuration per the aerodrome manual. This typically means:

| System | After-hours setting |
|---|---|
| Runway edge lighting | Off or minimum (pilot-activated if PALS installed) |
| Approach lighting / PAPI | Off (unless night RPT operations scheduled) |
| Taxiway lighting | Off |
| Apron floodlighting | Off or minimum security setting |
| Aerodrome beacon | Continue to operate dusk to dawn |
| Obstacle lighting | Continue to operate (if installed) |

If your aerodrome has a Pilot Activated Lighting System (PALS), confirm the system is active and functional so pilots can self-activate if they arrive after hours.

### Step 3 — Fuel security

Confirm the fuel storage area is:
- Locked and secure
- No fuel bowser left unsecured or connected to ground power
- No fuel spills visible
- Fire extinguisher in place and tagged

### Step 4 — Ground equipment

Confirm all ground support equipment is:
- Returned to its storage position
- Not left on the apron or taxiway
- Plugged in or fuelled as required for next day

### Step 5 — Access control

Walk the perimeter and confirm:
- All airside gates and access points are locked
- No vehicles are remaining airside that should not be there
- Fencing is intact — note and report any damage
- Security cameras (if installed) are operational

### Step 6 — Operations room

Before leaving the operations room:
- Complete the shift log (see below)
- Ensure all NOTAM filings are current
- File all inspection forms for the day
- Ensure the aerodrome manual, emergency plan, and contact lists are accessible for the next shift
- Charge radios and inspection equipment
- Check weather forecast for next morning — note any early call requirements

### Step 7 — NOTAMs

Review all active NOTAMs. Cancel any that have expired or no longer apply. If any defects found during the closing inspection require a NOTAM (and do not already have one), file now.

## Completing the shift log

The shift log is a legal record of aerodrome operations. A complete shift log entry for the closing of the aerodrome includes:

```
[TIME] Closing inspection commenced
[TIME] Closing inspection complete — all areas clear / defects as noted
[TIME] Lighting set to after-hours configuration
[TIME] Fuel area secured
[TIME] Apron equipment returned to storage
[TIME] All access gates secured
[TIME] Aerodrome closed to operations
[TIME] Shift log complete

Defects carried forward to next shift: [list or "Nil"]
NOTAMs active at close: [list NOTAM numbers or "Nil"]
Notes for incoming shift: [any relevant information]

Shift completed by: [your name]
```

> ⚠️ **Important:** Do not leave the aerodrome until the shift log is complete. A partial log creates ambiguity about what was and was not done.

## Handover — if you are relieving

If you are handing over to an incoming shift rather than closing for the night, the process is slightly different.

**Written handover (shift log)**
The incoming operator reads the shift log for the period you are handing over. Ensure all entries are complete before they arrive.

**Verbal handover**
Walk through the following with the incoming operator:

1. Movements completed during your shift — any anomalies or issues
2. Current weather and forecast
3. Active NOTAMs and their status
4. Any defects — current status, what action was taken, what is outstanding
5. Pending movements — expected arrivals or departures for the next period
6. Any unresolved issues that need follow-up
7. Equipment status — any vehicles, radios, or equipment that is unserviceable

Do not leave until the incoming operator has confirmed they have read the log and understood the handover. Sign and time the log.

---

## After-hours emergencies

Closing the aerodrome does not mean you are unavailable. As a FOO, you may be the primary point of contact for after-hours emergencies including medevac requests.

Ensure:
- Your after-hours contact number is listed in the aerodrome manual and emergency plan
- You know the procedure for reopening the aerodrome at short notice
- You know how to activate lighting remotely if that capability exists
- You have the contact details for emergency services, the airline duty manager, and your supervisor
