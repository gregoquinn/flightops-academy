# Airside access control

Controlling who and what enters the airside area is a fundamental security and safety responsibility. An unauthorised person on the movement area is both a security threat and a safety hazard. Effective access control prevents both.

---

## The security-controlled area

At a security-controlled airport, the airside area is divided into zones with different access requirements. For YSNF:

| Zone | Description | Access requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Landside | Terminal, public areas | General public access |
| Security-restricted area | Airside, apron, movement area | ASIC required or escorted |
| Movement area | Runway, taxiways, apron | ASIC + FOO authorisation |

---

## Access points

Access between landside and airside is controlled at designated access points — gates, doors, or vehicle entry points. Every access point must be:

- Lockable and locked when not in active use
- Monitored (physically or by CCTV) during operations
- Checked as part of the opening and closing inspection

If you find an access point unlocked or open when it should be secured, treat it as a security incident. Record it, secure it, and report it.

---

## Vehicle access control

Every vehicle entering the movement area must have:

1. **A current airside vehicle permit** — issued by the airport operator after the vehicle has been inspected and approved
2. **A driver who holds a current ASIC** — or is under continuous escort of an ASIC holder
3. **An amber flashing beacon** — visible from all sides when operating on the movement area
4. **Radio** — operational and on the CTAF (or as directed)

Vehicle permits are not permanent. They must be renewed periodically and are linked to specific vehicles — a permit for Vehicle A does not authorise Vehicle B.

---

## Challenging unidentified persons

If you encounter a person in the airside area who is not known to you and is not displaying an ASIC:

1. Approach them calmly
2. Identify yourself as aerodrome operations staff
3. Ask to see their ASIC
4. If they produce a valid ASIC, confirm it matches them and proceed
5. If they cannot produce an ASIC, escort them back to the landside area
6. If they are uncooperative or the situation feels threatening, do not confront them physically — withdraw to a safe position and call for police assistance

Document every challenge, including the outcome.

---

## Perimeter integrity

The security perimeter — the fence, wall, or other barrier that separates the airside from the landside — must be inspected regularly. During your opening and closing inspection, check:

- Fence line is intact with no gaps, breaks, or lifted sections
- Gates are in working order and lock correctly
- No ladders, boxes, or other objects have been placed against the fence (which could facilitate climbing)
- Vegetation has not grown to the point of obscuring the fence from inspection or providing cover for concealment

Report any perimeter defects to management immediately and assess whether the defect requires increased monitoring of the affected section.

---

## CCTV

If your aerodrome is equipped with CCTV, be familiar with:
- The camera locations and what each one covers
- How to review footage
- How long footage is retained
- The procedure for preserving footage in the event of an incident

CCTV footage is a valuable evidence source after security incidents. Know how to access it before you need it.

---

## Security incidents — reporting

Any of the following must be reported as a security incident:

- Unauthorised person found in the airside area
- Unattended bag or item in the airside area
- Person behaving suspiciously in or near the aerodrome
- Damaged or breached perimeter
- Missing or stolen ASIC
- Threat received (verbal, written, or electronic)
- Suspicious vehicle parked near the airside perimeter

Report to your supervisor and, for serious incidents, to the Australian Federal Police or Norfolk Island Police.

> 📋 **CASA reference:** Aviation Transport Security Act 2004, Part 3 — Security programs. Aviation Transport Security Regulations 2005 — access control and ASIC requirements.
