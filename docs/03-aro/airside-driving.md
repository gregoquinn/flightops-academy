# Airside driving

Driving on the movement area of a certified aerodrome is one of the highest-risk activities performed by aerodrome operations staff. Every vehicle on the movement area is a potential collision hazard for aircraft. This chapter addresses the AVIC0001 unit of competency — Drive on the airside.

---

## Regulatory basis

> 📋 **CASA reference:** MOS Part 139, Chapter 5 — Aerodrome works safety. CASR Part 139 — Aerodrome operator obligations. The aerodrome manual specifies the local rules for airside vehicle operations at YSNF.

---

## Airside vehicle permit

No vehicle may operate on the movement area of a certified aerodrome without a current **airside vehicle permit** issued by the aerodrome operator. The permit confirms that:

- The vehicle has been inspected and found suitable for airside use
- The vehicle is fitted with appropriate safety equipment
- The driver is authorised to operate that specific vehicle airside

Permits are vehicle-specific — a permit for Vehicle A does not authorise Vehicle B. Permits must be displayed on the vehicle and renewed periodically.

---

## Vehicle requirements

All vehicles operating on the movement area must meet the following minimum requirements:

| Requirement | Standard |
|---|---|
| Amber flashing beacon | Visible from all sides, operational at all times airside |
| Radio | Operational, tuned to CTAF, sufficient volume to hear transmissions with engine running |
| Hi-vis markings | Vehicle must be clearly visible to pilots and other drivers |
| Serviceable brakes, tyres, lights | Pre-operational check required before each airside entry |
| FOD prevention | No loose items, secure all equipment, clean tyres before entry |

---

## Pre-operational vehicle check

Before any vehicle enters the movement area, conduct the following checks:

**Exterior checks**
- Tyres — correct pressure, no damage, no mud or debris that could become FOD
- Lights — all operational including amber beacon
- No loose items on the roof, tray, or exterior
- Permit displayed and current

**Interior checks**
- Radio operational — conduct a radio check before entry
- Fuel sufficient for the inspection period
- Inspection forms, camera, FOD bag accessible
- Hi-vis vest on before exiting the vehicle airside

**Document the check** — most aerodrome manuals require a vehicle pre-operational check to be recorded in the shift log.

---

## Speed limits

Speed limits on the movement area are strictly enforced and exist for good reason — a vehicle travelling at 40km/h cannot stop in time if an aircraft appears unexpectedly.

| Area | Typical limit |
|---|---|
| Runway | 25 km/h maximum |
| Taxiway | 25 km/h maximum |
| Apron | 15 km/h near aircraft |
| General airside | As per aerodrome manual |

> ⚠️ **Important:** Always follow the speed limits specified in the aerodrome manual for YSNF. The limits above are typical — the aerodrome manual is the authoritative source for your specific aerodrome.

## Radio procedures for vehicles

Every vehicle operating on the movement area must maintain radio communication at all times. Before entering:

**Entry broadcast:**
> *"All stations Norfolk, [vehicle callsign], runway inspection, entering runway [number] at [time]."*

**During operations:**
Monitor the CTAF continuously. If an aircraft calls inbound, acknowledge your position and intentions:
> *"VH-XYZ, operations vehicle on runway [number], will vacate via [exit]."*

**Departure broadcast:**
> *"All stations Norfolk, [vehicle callsign], runway inspection complete, vacating [exit point], runway clear."*

**If your radio fails on the movement area:**
1. Stop the vehicle immediately
2. Move clear of the runway and taxiway as quickly as safely possible
3. Use a handheld backup radio if available
4. If no backup, use your mobile phone to call the operations room
5. Do not remain on an active runway without a functioning radio

---

## Runway entry and crossing procedures

Entering or crossing a runway requires the highest level of care. A runway incursion — any unauthorised entry onto a runway — is a serious incident reportable to the ATSB.

**Before entering a runway:**
1. Stop at the hold short line
2. Check visually — is the runway clear in both directions?
3. Check the radio — has any aircraft called inbound or on final?
4. Broadcast your intention — announce your entry
5. Confirm no conflicting traffic
6. Enter at the threshold or approved intersection only
7. Do not loiter — complete your task and vacate promptly

**Runway holding points**
Hold short markings consist of two solid yellow lines and two dashed yellow lines across the taxiway. The solid lines face the runway — you must not cross the solid lines without authorisation.

> ⚠️ **Important:** Even if you believe the runway is clear, always broadcast before entering. A pilot may be on a long final without having yet called. A broadcast costs 5 seconds. A runway incursion costs far more.

## Apron driving

The apron presents different hazards from the runway — slower speeds but higher density of activity.

**Key apron driving rules:**

- Yield to all aircraft at all times — aircraft always have right of way
- No vehicle approaches within the aircraft safety zone without ground handler supervision
- Do not drive behind a running jet engine or propeller
- Do not park in aircraft movement paths
- Do not park near fuel storage without authorisation
- Approach aircraft from the front quarter where the pilot can see you

**Jet blast and prop wash zones:**
Never position a vehicle directly behind a running engine. On an ATR 72, the prop wash during power-up is significant and can push a light vehicle. During engine start, all non-essential vehicles must be clear of the aircraft arc.

## Escorting vehicles airside

When escorting a non-permit vehicle or an unfamiliar driver onto the movement area:

1. Position your vehicle in front — you lead at all times
2. The escorted vehicle follows directly behind at a safe distance
3. Maintain radio communication throughout
4. Do not enter a runway or active taxiway without clearance and confirmation the escorted vehicle has stopped and is waiting
5. Brief the escorted driver before entry on all rules, speed limits, and what to do if your radio fails
6. On completion, escort the vehicle back out before you leave the airside

## Airside driving assessment requirements

The AVIC0001 unit of competency requires practical demonstration of airside driving skills. Competency is assessed against the following performance criteria:

| Performance criterion | What the assessor looks for |
|---|---|
| Pre-operational vehicle check | Systematic, documented check before entry |
| Radio operation | Correct broadcasts at entry and exit, monitoring during inspection |
| Movement area entry | Hold short, visual check, broadcast, confirm clear |
| Speed compliance | Consistent compliance with speed limits throughout |
| FOD identification | Active scanning, correct response to FOD found |
| Hazard identification | Recognises and responds appropriately to wildlife, lighting defects |
| Documentation | Correct completion of inspection forms during and after |
| Runway vacating | Prompt vacating, correct broadcast on exit |

> 💡 **Operational tip:** During your AVIC0001 practical assessment, the assessor is specifically watching to see whether you perform the entry broadcast and visual check as habits — not as things you do because someone is watching. Develop the habit before the assessment, not for the assessment.

## Common airside driving errors

The following errors are frequently identified in aerodrome operations audits and incident reports:

| Error | Consequence | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| Entering runway without broadcast | Runway incursion risk | Always broadcast — make it automatic |
| Driving too fast on apron | Unable to stop for unexpected aircraft movement | 15 km/h near aircraft, every time |
| Radio volume too low | Miss inbound aircraft call while engine running | Set volume before entry, test with radio check |
| Loose items in/on vehicle | FOD on movement area | Check vehicle exterior before every entry |
| Leaving vehicle airside unattended | Unauthorised person may access, FOD risk | Vehicle must be occupied or secured at all times airside |
| Crossing into contractor work area without authorisation | Works safety breach | Know the approved boundaries before you enter |
