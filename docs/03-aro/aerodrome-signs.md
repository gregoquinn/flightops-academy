# Aerodrome signs

Aerodrome signs provide mandatory instructions and information to pilots and vehicle drivers on the movement area. Unlike markings which are painted on the surface, signs are elevated boards that can be seen from a distance. As an ARO, you check aerodrome signs as part of every inspection.

---

## Regulatory basis

> 📋 **CASA reference:** MOS Part 139, Chapter 3 — Visual aids, Section 3.3 — Signs. ICAO Annex 14, Chapter 5 — Visual aids for navigation.

---

## Sign categories

Aerodrome signs fall into two main categories:

**Mandatory instruction signs**
Red background with white text. These signs require an action from the pilot or driver — they indicate where you must stop or what you must have before proceeding.

**Information signs**
Yellow background with black text, or black background with yellow text. These signs provide location or direction information.

---

## Mandatory instruction signs

### Runway designation signs

Located at runway/taxiway intersections, these signs display the runway designation(s) for the runway you are about to enter. Red background, white text.

Example: A sign showing **11-29** at the entrance to runway 11/29 from Taxiway Alpha.

**What to check:**
- Sign is present and clearly visible
- Text is legible — white on red, not faded
- Sign is upright and correctly positioned
- Sign illumination functional (if sign is internally lit)

### Runway hold position signs

These signs mark the holding position on taxiways at runway intersections. They display the runway designation of the runway being protected, with a red background.

**What to check:**
- Present at all runway/taxiway intersections
- Clearly visible from the approaching taxiway direction
- Text legible

### No entry signs

A white horizontal bar on a red background, indicating entry is prohibited. Used at runway exits to prevent vehicles or aircraft from entering a taxiway in the wrong direction.

---

## Information signs

### Location signs

Black background with yellow text. Identify the taxiway or runway on which you are currently located. Often combined with direction signs.

Example: **A** on a black background indicates you are on Taxiway Alpha.

### Direction signs

Yellow background with black text and arrows indicating the direction of taxiways or runways.

Example: **11 →** indicates runway 11 is to the right.

### Runway distance remaining signs

Black background with white numerals indicating the remaining landing distance in hundreds of metres. Posted at intervals along the runway.

---

## Sign inspection checklist

During every movement area inspection:

| Sign type | Check |
|---|---|
| Runway designation signs | Present, legible, upright, correct |
| Hold position signs | Present at each intersection, legible, red background clearly visible |
| No entry signs | Present where required, clearly visible |
| Location signs | Present and legible |
| Direction signs | Present, legible, arrows clearly visible |
| Distance remaining signs | Present, legible, correct sequence |

**Lighting (where signs are internally illuminated):**
- Check that all illuminated signs are functioning during low-light or night inspections
- A dark mandatory instruction sign (runway designation or hold position) is a significant defect — aircraft and vehicles cannot see it

**Common sign defects:**

| Defect | Likely cause | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Faded or illegible text | UV exposure, age | Report to maintenance, schedule replacement |
| Sign tilted or knocked | Vehicle contact | Record, arrange realignment |
| Broken or missing sign | Vehicle contact, vandalism | Report urgent defect, arrange replacement |
| Failed illumination | Lamp failure or electrical fault | Report, arrange repair, NOTAM if safety-critical |
| Obscured by vegetation | Grass or shrub growth | Report to maintenance for clearing |

---

## Signs vs markings — which takes precedence?

Where there is an apparent conflict between a sign and a marking:
- **Mandatory instruction signs always take precedence** over information signs
- **Hold position markings and hold position signs** together define the holding position — comply with whichever you reach first
- In any ambiguous situation, stop and clarify by radio before proceeding

## Practical identification exercise

Being able to identify signs quickly and correctly is a core ARO skill assessed in the AVIB0002 competency. Practise identifying these sign combinations:

| Sign description | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Red sign: **11-29** | You are about to enter runway 11/29 — stop and check for clearance |
| Red sign: **A** | Holding position for Taxiway Alpha |
| Yellow sign: **RWY 11 →** | Runway 11 is in the direction of the arrow |
| Black sign: **A** | You are on Taxiway Alpha |
| Red sign with white horizontal bar | No entry — do not proceed |
| Black sign: **9** | 900 metres of runway remaining |

> 💡 **Operational tip:** Walk the taxiway system at YSNF on foot during a quiet period early in your tenure. Read every sign, identify what it means, and confirm it matches the aerodrome diagram. Signs that are in the wrong position or display incorrect designations are genuine hazards that occasionally exist at older aerodromes.
